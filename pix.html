Pix!

Tagline

UX Design | Logo Design

Pix! is a concept app that facilitates group decision making and reduces indecision, allowing for more time to do activities rather than looking for activities. Imagine roadtripping with a group of friends and being able to easily come to a consensus on what activity you all do.


Problem: Groups often time have too many options to choose from and are unable to come up with a consensus. In addition, even if an individual has a recommendation, they feel too much social pressure to actually voice their opinion.


Solution: Pix! Minimizes the indecision within the group by systematically reducing options and provides an anonymous system for individuals to voice their recommendations.


Pix! started as a group assignment for school, but quickly envolved into a personal endeavor in which I used user ethnography skills such as interviews, personas, user journeys and probes.

PRIME OBJECTIVE

The primary goal of this app is to provide groups with the ability to reduce the amount of irrelevant suggestions and to quickly come to a consensus. An additional goal is to create an anonymous platform in which individuals can feel comfortable to voice their opinions and not feel the social anxiety that comes with deciding for the whole group.

Pix! isn’t a typical exploration app where you are presented a lengthy list of recommendations and hotspots, but more so it focuses on getting the user(s) to quickly decide on something and spend more time doing and less time searching.


Pix! LOGO

I've decided on the name 'Pix!'. I chose this name because of it's simple and sounds like the word 'picks'. I chose to go with a pixie/fairy as my product icon because I wanted this app to feel like a guide, or companion that works its ~magic~ to provide a solution. The idea of Pix! was strongly inspired by the pixie guide in Zelda: Phantom Hourglass, as it was based off the pixie that guided Link around. I also think a pixie is a unique visual representation instead of the typical location pin, map, or plane icons, that also added to the whimsical element of the app.


THE PROCESS

When creating Pix!, I made sure to follow the human-centered design process to ensure that what I was designing addressed a need that my users needed solved, not just what they wanted solved. The first step in the process was to create a design concept that was broad and addressed a new form of wayfinding. I, along with some classmates, decided that social traveling was an interesting concept and wanted to explore road trips. For this project, my group and I followed a pretty standard process of research to ideation to design solution, with our workflow outlined below.

This is a more detailed explaination of our workflow. We started by choosing our initial design frame and worked that into a broad concept. Then we did some quick interviews to narrow down our concept and get a better understanding of who we are designing for. After developing our user groups, we performed indepth interviews and communicated what the main insights we got from them were.

The Interviews & Insights

The two user group partipants I interviewed were friends of mine whom I know are both avid trip planners and well-versed travellers. From the observations I made from the interviews, I was able to deduce the insight that people often waste a lot of time on road trips trying to decide what the group should do, and in addition, even if an individual has a suggestion, they are afraid to voice it in fear of the group rejecting it.

Both of the interview participants fell into the characteristics of our user groups. The quotes stated above were the statements that I observed to be particularly interesting, and lead to the deduction my insights.

Identifying & Alleviating Pain Points

After we conducted interviews and accumulated insights, we created user journey maps to better understand areas of the road trip experience we could improve. We were able to to identify two pain points: logistical planning and deciding on nearby exploration. At this point, my group gave me complete autonomy on the final design solution and did not contribute much more. I decided that the issue of deciding nearby exploration was an interesting problem to tackle and brainstormed Pix!, a concept app that essential makes a randomized decision for the group based on a ballot system.

The image above is Varsha's User Journey Map. The x-axis describes the chronological activites of her road trip, and the y-axis represents her happiness levels during those activities. From the user journey, two pain points became obvious and were ciricled in red.

HI-FIDELITY SCREENS


On Boarding

User onboarding is the user’s initial experience with your brand and product. It spans from the moment someone starts to sign up for your product, until the moment they realize how your product is going to improve their life. I wanted to ensure that users understand what Pix! does, how it will benefit them, and ultimately show them how to use the app.

Creating Groups

After being greeted with the onboarding screens, the user is then asked to choose or create the group of friends whom they would like to survey. I chose a tile layout because it was important that the interface remain as minimal as possible. By minimizing buttons on the screen, the user can focus on getting the task done and not get distracted by different features.

The user is presented with the option of either creating a new group, selecting and existing group or editing the members of a new group.

This screen shows the user creating a new group. The user is prompted to create a name for the new group and can either add new users or select from pre-existing users. The user is given positive confirmation when someone is added as indicated by the green check mark.

This screen shows the user editing and existing group. The user has the ability to add new members or remove exisiting members.

After the user has selected a group, the group is highlighted in green indicated that it has been selected. The user can then click "choose group" to continue.
