Pix!

#ux design   #ui design

The Problem

Traveling as a group is more fun, but how might we make group decision making more harmonious?

Everyone enjoys traveling. The experience of seeing exciting places, eating exotic foods, and trying new activities through traveling is a experience that every person wants to enjoy; but the experience is even better when you have someone to share it with.


Because of this, people often like to travel in groups when doing road trips or traveling to a new place; but although a larger group means more fun, it also means more disputes when it comes to making decisions: from what music to play in the car or where everyone wants to eat for dinner.

Background

Pix! was created as a result of a class project where I, along with a group of fellow students, was assigned to think of ways to improve the way finding experience. The end goal of the project was to present a deliverable that explained our ideation method, as well as our user research. Our group utilized strategies such affinity mapping, interviews, and persona creation; as well as tools such as Illustrator and Sketch to create our Hi-fidelity mockups. In the end our group created several possible products to improve the way finding experience of group travelers and roadtrippers. Ultimately, I took it into my own hands to develop one our ideas into a more fleshed out product: Pix, whose overall goal is to reduce the indecision in the group way finding process.

The Design Process

When we started our project, we were faced with the very broad question of: how can we improve the way finding experience. Because of the expansive nature of our task, our group had an extended designing process in which we did quick and dirty interviews and affinity mapping to identify main themes within way fining and narrow our research question.

After the initial rounds of user research, we decided that the idea of social traveling and road trips was an interesting concept that we wanted to approach. After establishing that we wanted to reduce the tension created from traveling in groups, we were able to define the characteristics of our target user group. From here we performed interviews on people who matched our criteria and compiled our information.

User Research and Methods

Our research revealed that the predominant way people explored in groups was by having a main group leader(s) plan an itinerary, or by having individuals search and explore the area using social media sites such as Yelp! or Instagram. Using our insights and observations, we created personas to better understand who we are designing for and to better empathize with the our rr statements. Some of the most impactful insights that we accrued from our research were:

  1. Travelers want to find a balance between a well planned trip that still maintains a feel of spontaneity.

  2. Typical tools for discovering new places when traveling often present an overwhelming amount of information, making it difficult to decide on a place.

  3. When possible, travelers like to consult friends who have previously visited the destination for their recommendations. Additionally, there seems to be an irrational fear of asking locals for recommendations.

  4. There is a fear of voicing a suggestion because of the feeling of "choosing for the entire group", and its possible consequences.

A large part in constructing these personas, was considering what parts of traveling our interviewees enjoyed the most, and dislicked the most.


After developing personas, we used them in conjunction with our interview information to map out a user journey for Varsha. Based on her responses during our interview, we mapped out the activities and events based on chronological order on the x-axis, and gauged her relatives feelings towards these events and mapped them in order of most unhappy to most enjoyable on the y-axis.

Design Opportunities

After analyzing our personas and user journeys, we found two design opportunities that we wanted to address:

  1. Using social media to foster social traveling: the idea of using social media platforms to add spontaneous activities into the adventure.

  2. How might we reduce the time wasted on deciding where to go when traveling with a large group, such as on a road trip or retreat.

Ideation

Idea 1: Geolocation based suggestions “left behind” by friends who also travelled the same/similar route.

A concept app that allows for the users to post their own travel route and also view the travel route of others. This would allow travelers to create an initial plan, and add ~spontaneously~ to the itinerary as they go. To better facilitate this spontaneity, the app would introduce the ability for travelers to place “drops” based on their geolocation and these messages will appear on their travel route for other travelers to pick up. “Drops” are basically recommendations that are pinned to a specific location and can be accessed when a traveller enters its radius. The “drop” feature allows users to get recommendations for friends without directly asking them, and allows locals to contribute their recommendations anonomysly


Idea 2: Decision pressure relief through collaborative choosing, and reduced options.

Introducing Pix!

Pix! isn’t a typical exploration app where you are presented a lengthy list of recommendations and hotspots, but more so it focuses on getting the user(s) to quickly decide on something and spend more time doing and less time searching.


The primary goal of this app is to provide groups with the ability to reduce the amount of irrelevant suggestions and to quickly come to a consensus. An additional goal is to create an anonymous platform in which individuals can feel comfortable to voice their opinions and not feel the social anxiety that comes with deciding for the whole group.

Minimal Layout Prevents Distractions and Encourages Quick Responses

Pix! is void of any extraneous or distracting features, its straight forward user flow encourages the users to quickly collaborate and make a decision. The UI's lack of bells and whistles allows the user to get their task done quickly and get back to enjoying their trip.

Quick Access to Persons in Taveling Group

Groups are displayed in a manner that allows for them to be selected quickly. In addition, persons can added to existing groups and (ideally) would be identified by bluetooth or similar technology.

Easily Inputed and Searchable Group Suggestions

Instead of having an expansive list of options to choose from, such as one would see on Yelp, the app presents a more limited variety of nearby spots. Additionally, this creates an environment that rewards those who have done prior research and already have an idea of where they want to go, because they can easily type in a suggestion.

Blameless Ballot Style Voting

A large barrier in the decision making process is individual's fear of choosing for the group, and the group not enjoying the place that was chosen. By implementing a a ballot system that treats each vote as a tally and randomly selecting from those tally percents, no one individual can be held accountable for the group's choice.
