Background

bCourses is the UC Berkeley specific version of the web application canvas. The purpose of the web application is to assist instructors and students with the process of submitting assignments, communicating changes and grade and relaying course information. As a personal project, I redesigned bCourses in a quick 1 week design sprint.

The Challenge

Seeing as bCourses is vital to the academic success of students, I challenged myself with:

  • understanding the needs and frustrations of UC Berkeley students with regards to their interactions with bCourse
  • creating a fleshed out redesign of the bCourse web application

User Research and Data Analysis

Because bCourse is specific to UC Berkeley students, I decided on two main modes of user research:

  • creating a user survey that sought to understand user’s logistical interactions with bCourses
  • interviews with UC Berkeley students

I created the survey using google forms and sent it out via emails and facebook messages; additionally I asked friends to help spread the survey. From a survey of around 25 UC Berkeley students, I was able to identify:

  • primary use cases for bCourses
  • frequency of use
  • primary mode of access
