Helix

#branding   #logo design

As part of Innovative Design, a student run design consultancy at UC Berkeley, I had the honor of working with Nolan in creating a logo and brand image for the club Helix.

Meeting with Nolan, he explained Helix’s goal: increasing healthcare equity through medical education. Further more, Nolan emphasized how the desrepency between POC in medical positions often results in POC not feeling comfortable with going to the doctor’s office for a check-up and how their club aspires to inspire people from these communities to follow a path in the medical field.


I sketched and then mocked up a set of divergent concepts to get a sense of direction for the brand.

After viewing the sketches and mock-ups and relaying them back to his club, Nolan decided that the icons for 01 (Chromosome Recombination) and 02 (Abstract Helix) would be best.


Together, we worked to create a color pallate that best suited the club. Together, we decided that primary colors best represented Helix's "back to basics" outlook; and it helps that blue and gold are Cal's school color.

In the end, the Helix team decided to take my input and finish the logo in-house. You can see their final interation of the logo at their site.
