Plant Parenthood

#ux design   #ui design   #interaction design

The Challenge

Not everyone has a green thumb; and tending towards house plants can be a difficult process, especially in the hectic lives we live everyday. How might we be more aware to the needs of our little plant buddies?!

Background

This summer, I sub-letted at a friend’s apartment and was tasked with making sure that her new plants stay alive and well over the course of my stay. Considering that I take care of my own little doggy at home, I thought that this would be a small feat for me… And boy was I wrong. A few weeks later, I stumbled upon a YouTube video by CommunityChannel (Natalie Tran) that perfectly explained this feeling of []. I took it upon myself to try to understand why there is a disconnect when it comes to tending to plants, and try to find a way for plant owners to be more compassionate towards their plants.

A Lean UX Process

Being a personal project, I was limited in the amount of available resources that I was usually accustomed to while doing user research. As a result, I felt as though I needed to think more “outside the box” and force myself to consider more outlandish ideas when doing affinity mapping, or preparing potential interview questions. Additionally, I was required to conduct a large amount of interviews on my own; and as a result, interview candidates were often chosen from a closer group of friends who loosely met a criteria, rather than a more expansive and diverse group.


With a general idea of what I information I needed to tackle the challenge, I started off producing an affinity map to understand what concepts are important to the user.
