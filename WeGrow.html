Background

Through the class IEOR 170: Human Factors and, I was given the chance to work with a great group to tackle an issue that was dear to our hearts: food sustainability and food accessibility in the Bay Area. The project was intended to be a semester long [trek] and was culminated with a final prototype, a presentation poster and a video. Throughout the semester, our team explored with many product solutions ranging from physical products to digital software solutions; ultimately our group decided that the best solution was to create a digital application. Our design process required us to do user ethnography in the form of multiple interviews, and the creation of personas and user journey maps based on the observations from the aforementioned interviews. The creation of the actual design solution was created using Sketch and prototyped using InVision.

The Solution: Introducing WeGrow

Pix! isn’t a typical exploration app where you are presented a lengthy list of recommendations and hotspots, but more so it focuses on getting the user(s) to quickly decide on something and spend more time doing and less time searching.


The primary goal of this app is to provide groups with the ability to reduce the amount of irrelevant suggestions and to quickly come to a consensus. An additional goal is to create an anonymous platform in which individuals can feel comfortable to voice their opinions and not feel the social anxiety that comes with deciding for the whole group.

Minimal Layout Prevents Distractions and Encourages Quick Responses

Pix! is void of any extraneous or distracting features, its straight forward user flow encourages the users to quickly collaborate and make a decision. The UI's lack of bells and whistles allows the user to get their task done quickly and get back to enjoying their trip.

Quick Access to Persons in Taveling Group

Groups are displayed in a manner that allows for them to be selected quickly. In addition, persons can added to existing groups and (ideally) would be identified by bluetooth or similar technology.

Easily Inputed and Searchable Group Suggestions

Instead of having an expansive list of options to choose from, such as one would see on Yelp, the app presents a more limited variety of nearby spots. Additionally, this creates an environment that rewards those who have done prior research and already have an idea of where they want to go, because they can easily type in a suggestion.

Blameless Ballot Style Voting

A large barrier in the decision making process is individual's fear of choosing for the group, and the group not enjoying the place that was chosen. By implementing a a ballot system that treats each vote as a tally and randomly selecting from those tally percents, no one individual can be held accountable for the group's choice.
